Retro-Box

HARDWARE SOFTWARE 2026

The idea

Streaming killed the best part of TV: not choosing. Retro-Box brings back channel-surfing — a Raspberry Pi 4 feeds a 1990s Sony PVM broadcast monitor a lineup of "channels" built from shows on a hard drive. Turn it on and something is already playing. Flip channels and you land mid-episode, just like real broadcast TV.

How it works

  • Raspberry Pi 4 outputs composite video to the PVM for the authentic CRT look.
  • Shows live on an attached hard drive, organized into themed channels.
  • A scheduler decides what's "on air" per channel, so flipping back to a channel resumes the broadcast where it would naturally be.
  • Channel changes trigger a static/tuning effect for the full vintage feel.

What was hard

  • Getting clean composite output and correct geometry on the PVM.
  • Making "what's on right now" deterministic so channels feel like live broadcasts, not playlists.
  • Fast, seamless channel switching on modest Pi hardware.

Built with

  • Raspberry Pi 4
  • Sony PVM-9042Q
  • Linux
  • Python
  • Composite video
